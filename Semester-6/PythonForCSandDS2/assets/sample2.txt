Cuttack, often called the "Millennium City," is a historic urban center in Odisha founded in 989 CE by King Nrupa Kesari and renowned for its exquisite silver filigree work, which earned it the nickname the "Silver City." Situated between the Mahanadi and Kathajodi rivers, the city served as the erstwhile capital of Odisha until 1948 when the capital shifted to Bhubaneswar, yet it remains the state's commercial and judicial capital hosting the Orissa High Court.  As the birthplace of Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose, Cuttack blends a rich heritage of ancient forts like Barabati Fort with vibrant cultural celebrations such as Durga Puja and Baliyatra, while its layout of narrow lanes and markets reflects its origins as a military cantonment derived from the Sanskrit word Kataka.
